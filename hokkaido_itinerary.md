# Hokkaido, Japan — 10-Day Itinerary
### Four Intermediate-Advanced Skiers, Peak Powder, and a Worrying Amount of Ramen
**Dates:** December–January | **Base:** Sapporo → Rusutsu → Sapporo | **Flying:** SFO

---

## Pre-Trip: Getting There

**Flight:** SFO → Tokyo (Haneda HND preferred, or Narita NRT) → New Chitose Airport (CTS), Sapporo. JAL and ANA have the best connections. Budget **$900–$1,300/person round-trip** booked 2–3 months out. Total travel time: ~16–18 hours door-to-door.

**Pro tip:** Book seats together for the SFO–Tokyo leg (~10 hours). That's where you actually want to sleep, not the 90-minute domestic hop to CTS.

---

## Day 1 — Land, Decompress, Eat Ramen (This Is the Way)

**Afternoon: Arrive at New Chitose Airport (CTS)**

Welcome to Japan. Clear customs, grab cash from the 7-Eleven ATM in arrivals (accepts foreign cards reliably — load up, this country still runs on yen), and pick up a **Kitaca IC transit card** at the JR ticket machine. Load ¥5,000 on it. You'll use this on every train, subway, and bus for the rest of the trip, so you're never fishing for coins again.

Take the **JR Rapid Airport** to Sapporo Station (~40 min, ¥1,150/person). It's punctual, comfortable, and the antithesis of every BART experience you've collectively suffered.

**Recommended hotels (mid-range):**
- **Cross Hotel Sapporo** (~¥12,000–16,000/room/night) — central, clean, great access to Susukino
- **Hotel Monterey Sapporo** (~¥10,000–14,000) — slightly quieter alternative, still well-located

**Evening: Ramen Alley (Sapporo Ramen Yokocho)**

Drop bags. Walk to **Sapporo Ramen Yokocho** (すすきのラーメン横丁) in Susukino — a narrow alley, maybe ten tiny stalls, each seating a dozen people under warm light while snow falls outside. Sapporo's signature style is **miso ramen**: rich pork-and-miso broth, corn, butter, and springy noodles. It will make you question every cup of instant ramen you've put in your body since college.

This is dinner and also the moment someone says "I can't believe we're actually here." Budget ~¥1,200/bowl.

After ramen: wander Susukino. The neon, the izakayas, the warmth spilling out into the cold. Keep it early — jet lag will collect its debt tonight whether you fight it or not.

**Day 1 budget:** ~¥5,000–7,000/person

## Day 2 — Sapporo: Seafood, Beer History, and Grilled Lamb

**Morning: Nijo Market (二条市場)**

Five-minute walk from most central hotels. This is a compact outdoor-indoor seafood market where Hokkaido's best gets sold at wooden stalls before it can escape to a restaurant.

Order an **ikura don** (salmon roe rice bowl) or a **kaisendon** (mixed seafood bowl) with sea urchin, scallop, and crab. Yes, this is breakfast. Yes, it's absolutely appropriate. ~¥1,500–2,500/person.

**Afternoon: Sapporo Beer Museum**

Non-negotiable. The **Sapporo Beer Museum** (サッポロビール博物館) is Japan's oldest brewery (established 1876), housed in a gorgeous red brick landmark. Admission is **free**; the **Premium Tour** (~¥1,000/person) includes tastings: Black Label on draft, classic Sapporo, and seasonal releases.

Next door: the **Sapporo Beer Garden** has an all-you-can-eat **Jingisukan** (ジンギスカン, "Genghis Khan") option — grilled lamb and vegetables on a convex iron dome, unlimited, for ~¥2,800/person + drinks. It's loud, meaty, and the kind of communal meal that a friend group does extremely well.

**Evening: Susukino Izakaya Night**

- **Daruma Honten** (だるま本店) — legendary Jingisukan, almost always a line, always worth it
- **Kaitaro** (かい太朗) — fresh Hokkaido seafood izakaya style; order the scallop, the squid, the roe

After dinner, hit a whisky bar in Susukino as a preview of what's coming later in the trip. Bars carrying Nikka single malts are all over this neighborhood.

**Day 2 budget:** ~¥9,000–13,000/person

## Day 3 — Sapporo → Rusutsu: First Contact with the Mountain

**Morning: Final City Morning**

Slow coffee at a **kissaten** (old-school Japanese coffee shop). Then a supply run:
- Hand warmers (*kairo*) — ¥200/pack at any konbini, carry at least 4/day
- Sunscreen (altitude + snow reflection = sunburn that sneaks up on you)
- Konbini snacks for jacket pockets (onigiri, energy bars, Pocky)

**Midday: Transfer to Rusutsu (~90 min southwest of Sapporo)**

- **Donan Bus** from Sapporo Station (~¥2,500/person one-way, book in advance)
- **Resort shuttle** — check rusutsu.com for seasonal shuttle schedules
- **Rental car** — 4WD compact (~¥7,000–9,000/day, snow tires mandatory and included)

**Afternoon: Check In + First Runs**

**Rusutsu Resort** — three interconnected mountains (West Mountain, East Mountain, Isola), 37+ km of runs, exceptional tree skiing, and a fraction of the crowds you'd find in Niseko 45 minutes away. Right call.

Check in to **Rusutsu Resort Hotel & Convention**. Multiple on-site dining, onsen, ski-in/ski-out access.

> **Pro tip — Buy the 25-Hour Flexible Lift Ticket:** ¥33,100/person online vs. ¥12,000/day online (¥16,200 walk-up). For 5 days of skiing at ~5 hours each, you spend ¥33,100 vs. ¥60,000. That's ¥27,000 saved per person — your crab dinner and then some. Buy at rusutsu.com before you travel.

Head to the **rental shop immediately**. Reserve equipment sizes online in advance. Get helmets — tree skiing + Japow at speed = mandatory helmet energy.

**Evening: Irodori Restaurant (Resort)**

Japanese set dinner using Hokkaido-sourced ingredients. Good call for a travel-day evening.

**Day 3 budget:** ~¥16,000–22,000/person (transport + equipment rental + dinner; lift ticket pre-purchased)

## Day 4 — Full Send: East + West Mountains

**All Day: The Mountain**

**Morning — East Mountain:** Gondola from the hotel base. Work through groomed blue/black runs to warm up legs that forgot what skiing felt like.

**Midday — West Mountain:** Rusutsu's glory. Tree runs in the birch glades — untracked powder, silence except for the occasional whooping. This is what you came for.

**Lunch on mountain:** **Akaigoya** (mid-mountain) — miso ramen, katsu curry rice, corn soup. ~¥1,200–1,500. Go 11:30am or after 1:30pm to avoid peak crowds.

**Afternoon — Isola:** Third peak, quieter, best for off-piste exploration and sessionable features.

**Night skiing:** Select lifts open until ~8–9pm. Lit runs, empty mountain, snow still falling. Do it at least once.

**Apres-ski:** Resort bar. Establish the **Fall of the Day Award** — mandatory for a group of four who've known each other long enough to roast without mercy.

**Day 4 budget:** ~¥5,000–7,000/person (food + drinks)

---

## Day 5 — Powder Day Protocol + Kutchan Evening

**Morning: West Mountain Trees (Priority)**

If it snowed overnight — and in December–January in Hokkaido, it often will — get to the West Mountain glades before anyone else. Untracked powder has a time limit. First chair opens ~8–8:30am.

**Evening: Kutchan Town**

Taxi or bus to **Kutchan** (~20 min). Lower prices than the resort, local crowd, real izakayas.

Yakitori, edamame, Hokkaido craft beer on tap, and the warmth of a room full of people who also spent the day on a mountain. ~¥3,000–4,000/person for a full evening with drinks.

Stock up: Kutchan has a grocery store and konbini if you want snacks for the hotel.

**Day 5 budget:** ~¥6,000–8,000/person

---

## Day 6 — Onsen Day (Or Powder Emergency Override)

**Mode A: Onsen Day (Highly Recommended)**

**Rusutsu Resort Onsen** is on-site. For something more atmospheric: taxi to a local **ryokan** for day-use bathing (~¥1,000–1,500/person).

Procedure: mandatory shower first (read the signs), then ease into water that is much hotter than it looks. Alternate between indoor and outdoor baths (rotenburo). No phones. Stare at the snow. Feel every muscle unknot. This is genuinely one of the best things about Hokkaido winter.

**Mode B: Powder Override**

If significant snowfall happened overnight, the onsen waits. Untracked powder doesn't.

**Evening: Kaiseki Dinner**

Multi-course Japanese dinner at Irodori or a nearby ryokan (~¥5,000–8,000/person). A parade of small, exquisite Hokkaido-sourced dishes. Everyone goes quiet in the best way.

**Day 6 budget:** ~¥5,000–10,000/person

---

## Day 7 — Rusutsu Finale: Run the Hits

**All Day: Your Mountain, Your Runs**

Run your favorites. That one run someone has been trying to ski cleanly all week: today is the day. West Mountain trees, one more time. Group photo at the mountain top — you've said you'd do it every day. Today it is actually mandatory.

Stop by 3pm. Pack. Confirm checkout. Make sure no one left a glove on a chairlift.

**Evening: Farewell Rusutsu Dinner**

**The Steakhouse** at Rusutsu Hotel uses Hokkaido beef, which is exceptional. Or return to your favorite Kutchan izakaya for a proper farewell round.

**Day 7 budget:** ~¥8,000–12,000/person

## Day 8 — Rusutsu → Yoichi → Otaru → Sapporo

*The natural route home passes directly through two excellent stops.*

> ⚠️ **CRITICAL NIKKA NOTE:** The Yoichi Distillery **closes December 23 – January 7** for the New Year holiday. If your trip falls in this window, see the Alternative Plan below. Tours require **advance reservations** — book at nikka.com/en/distilleries before you leave home.

**Morning: Check Out of Rusutsu → Bus to Kutchan (~20 min)**

Leave by 8:30–9am. Then take the **JR Hakodate Main Line** east toward Sapporo.

**Late Morning: Yoichi — Nikka Whisky Distillery**
*JR: Kutchan → Yoichi, ~30 min, ~¥690*

Founded in 1934 by Masataka Taketsuru — trained in Scotland, married a Scottish woman named Rita, chose Yoichi because its cold, damp climate was the closest thing to the Highlands he could find in Japan.

**Free guided tour** (~70 min, reservation required): pot stills, aging warehouses, Taketsuru's original home, and a museum that's more interesting than you'll expect. Tours run approximately hourly, 9:15am–3:30pm (last admission).

**Tasting room:** Nikka single malts, blends, and the famous **Coffey Grain Whisky** — ¥500–1,500 for a tasting set. Designated driver gets apple juice, moral authority, and first pick of the next restaurant. Decide before the tour.

Allow 2.5 hours total.

**Afternoon: Otaru**
*JR: Yoichi → Otaru, ~25 min, ~¥400*

Former herring-trade port town. Snow-covered canal. Gas lamps. Early-20th-century stone warehouses. In December, the **Otaru Snow Light Path Festival** (雪あかりの路) lines the canal with snow lanterns — check exact dates for your visit.

- **Otaru Canal** — walk it, photograph it, required
- **Sushi lunch:** Otaru is one of Hokkaido's best sushi towns. **Masazushi** (政寿司) on the covered shopping street is a mid-range institution
- **LeTAO** — Famous Hokkaido cheesecake. The **double fromage** is one of the best things you'll eat this trip. One per person, no sharing
- **Otaru Beer** (小樽ビール) — German-style craft brewery near the canal, half-liter of weizen while watching snow fall

**Evening: Sapporo**
*JR: Otaru → Sapporo, ~40 min, ~¥750*

Check in. Light dinner — konbini run or a bowl of ramen near the hotel. Long day, great day.

**Day 8 budget:** ~¥8,000–13,000/person

## Day 9 — Final Sapporo Day: Food, Shopping, and Savoring It

**Morning: Odori Park + TV Tower**

Walk **Odori Park** (大通公園) — in December, home of the **Sapporo White Illumination** festival. The **Sapporo TV Tower** observation deck at the east end has excellent city views (~¥700).

**Mid-Morning: Tanukikoji Shopping Street**

Covered shopping arcade, warm and several blocks long. Souvenir central:
- **Royce' Chocolate** — potato-chip chocolate bar, buy multiple boxes
- **Nikka From the Barrel** miniatures or a full bottle — one of the best value whiskies in the world
- Hokkaido dairy products, local crafts, Sapporo beer gift sets

**Lunch: Soup Curry**

Sapporo invented this. Fragrant, thin curry broth with roasted Hokkaido vegetables and chicken. Spice level customizable — go at least a 5 or 6.

- **Soup Curry GARAKU** — layered broth, Hokkaido vegetables, go 11:30am or after 1:30pm
- **Suage+** — fried vegetables, excellent aromatics, modern interior

~¥1,200–2,000/person.

**Afternoon: Craft Beer**

**Distant Shores Beer** — American craft taproom in Sapporo. The place to have the "what was the best run of the trip" argument over rotating IPAs and lagers.

**Evening: Farewell Crab Dinner**

Hokkaido crab — king (taraba), snow (zuwai), or hairy (kegani). Do it properly.

**Kani Doraku** or **Kani Shogun** in Sapporo: multi-course crab dinners or all-you-can-eat (~¥5,000–8,000/person). You will be embarrassed by how much you eat. This is expected and endorsed.

Late night: Susukino, one final time.

**Day 9 budget:** ~¥12,000–18,000/person

**Morning: The Efficient Japanese Exit**

**JR Rapid Airport** from Sapporo Station → CTS: 40 minutes, ¥1,150. Allow 2 hours before your international flight.

## Travel Tips

**Buy the 25-Hour Lift Ticket Online**
At ¥33,100 online vs. ¥16,200/day walk-up, the math is clear. Buy at rusutsu.com before you leave. A 35-hour option (limited availability) exists if you want extra buffer.

**Ski Gear Shipping (Takkyubin / 宅急便)**
Kuroneko Yamato will ship ski bags from Sapporo to Rusutsu Resort for ~¥2,000–3,000 per bag. Do this. Ski bags on a crowded JR train are miserable.

**Book Nikka Yoichi in Advance**
Tours require reservations and have limited group sizes. Book at nikka.com/en/distilleries before departure. Verify holiday closure dates against your specific travel window.

**Cash Is King**
Keep ¥10,000+ on hand at all times. Many ramen shops, izakayas, and market stalls are cash-only. 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards reliably throughout Japan.

**Konbini Culture Is Real**
Japanese convenience stores (7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart) sell legitimately good food. Fresh onigiri, hot oden, cold Sapporo for ~¥220/can. The best pregame setup in the world. Budget ¥500–800/day for konbini runs.

**Cold Weather Reality Check**
December–January: -5°C to -20°C on the mountain, -3°C to -10°C in Sapporo. Layering system: merino base layer, fleece mid-layer, waterproof shell. Goggles, neck gaiter, liner gloves under ski gloves. Chemical hand warmers in every pocket.

**Language**
Rusutsu has decent English for an international resort. Sapporo is manageable — Google Translate camera mode handles menus. Three phrases: **sumimasen** (excuse me), **arigatou gozaimasu** (thank you), **oishii** (delicious). You'll use that last one constantly.

**Shinkansen Option (If Plans Extend)**
Sapporo to Tokyo via limited express + Shinkansen: ~4.5 hours, ~¥22,000–27,000 one way. Not worth it on this 10-day schedule but useful to know.
